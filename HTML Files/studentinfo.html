Distributing files to students incites a paperless classroom environment. Deforestation and increased trash are results of the excessive use of paper, which has negative consequences on the environment. Teachers can greatly lessen their ecological impact and instil the value of sustainable practices in students by switching to digital files. In this way, incorporating technology promotes ethical behavior and inspires students to embrace eco-friendly practices. Teachers can give their pupils comprehensive and well-organized materials by sending files. Digital files are conveniently kept, accessed, and searchable, in contrast to conventional ways where students could misplace or lose essential documents. Students can keep their device folder structures nicely organized so they always have the resources they need close at hand. This simplified method improves students' capacity for subject review and revision, resulting in better learning results.
